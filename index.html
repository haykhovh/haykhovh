Published Research

"History and politics" Academic journal, October 2020

About the Motivation theory of salary

Yerevan, Armenia

The article aims to challenge the salary motivation theory, in particular, it discusses the motivational factor of salary, the impact of salary on employee satisfaction and its impact on productivity. Numerous studies show that wages do not have a significant impact on either employees' pay satisfaction or their job. For years, salary has been considered as one of the most important factors in determining labor efficiency. However, this work shows that wages should be interpreted mainly as a market-economic category, not as a managerial-motivational factor. High salary makes work attractive for highly qualified professionals, but it is not in itself a tool for raising qualifications and/or capacity building.

Labor efficiency in Armeniaâ€¤ Conclusions about lost economic growth

Yerevan, Armenia

Numerous quantitative and qualitative indicators can be considered during the analysis to find out the causatives and the resultatives of a certain economic situation. One such indicator is the "efficiency of economic activity of the population", which can be described by quantifying the labor efficiency. The labor efficiency, According to the GDP method, is the gross domestic product created per employee or the gross domestic product created by the employee per working hour. The analysis of these criteria within the framework of the article allows us to consider the dynamics of labor efficiency in the Republic of Armenia, as well as enables us to show the marginal GDP of an hour of work through quantitative calculation. Accordingly, in comparison with the efficiency of the labor force and the working hours in Armenia, certain ideological issues are raised regarding the lost economic growth of the Republic of Armenia. Co-author of the article: Siranush Poghosyan, PhD.

The essence of the negative interest rateâ€¤ Prerequisites for the introduction of a negative interest rate in Armenia

Yerevan, Armenia

After the financial and economic crisis of 2008 banking systems around the world adopted a policy of negative interest rates. Moreover, both real and nominal interest rates can be negative. If in the past negative interest rates were used only in critical situations, today some countries have introduced negative interest rates in the long term. The hyper concentrated economy of RA, the possible implementation of Basic Income and the Resettlement of Border towns in Armenia

Stepanakert, Artsakh, Armenia

After the collapse of the USSR, deep crises began in the member states of the union, which had a serious impact on the socio-economic development of countries. Today, the consequences of this crisis are obvious in the context of the balanced territorial development of the Republic of Armenia. Both socially and economically, the economy of the Republic of Armenia is concentrated in the capital Yerevan, and the situation in the provinces and border regions of the country remains difficult. This article discusses the practice of using countercyclical tools known in the field of finance and macroeconomics, and their projection in socio-economic problems. In particular, it is proposed to include the idea of basic income and thereby stimulate the growth of the resident population in the regions. This approach can replace existing tax incentives in some regions.

The multiplier effect in monopolized economies

Yerevan, Armenia

The state manages the aggregate demand through fiscal and monetary policies, and in case of fiscal policy the idea of budget multiplier is revealed. The main component of the budget multiplier is the marginal propensity to consume. However, many other factors directly or indirectly affect the multiplier. These coefficients are derived from elements of aggregate demand. One of these elements, investment, is dependent on income, which is characterized by a coefficient of flexibility Î³. This ratio varies from company to company, and it is proposed to divide the coefficient for analysis depending on the dominant or normal position of the company in the market. An analysis of factors affecting multiplier in monopolized economies allows to estimate the magnitude of the multiplier effect lost due to monopolization. The more monopolized the economy is, the less effective the multiplier works.
